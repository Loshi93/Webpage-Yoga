Amazing Yoga Asanas that will tone and treat your entire Body

1. Padahastasana - For Flat Tummy

This asana is a basic Vinyasa style asana. It quite literally means hand to foot pose. ‘Pada’ in Sanskrit means foot, ‘hastha’ stands for hands, and ‘asana’ means pose. This asana is pronounced as Paa-da-has-thaa-sana.

How to do it?

    1. Stand straight. Inhale and lift your arms above your head.
    2. Now exhale, and gently bend your body forward. Let your palms touch your feet. It might be difficult at first, so let your arms reach as far as possible. Your body shouldn’t be strained at any point.
    3. Your trunk should be placed close to your legs. Your forehead should touch your knees. But this comes with practice, and might be difficult initially. Just remember to listen to your body, and stop when it asks you to.
    4. Stay in this position for a few seconds as you breathe normally. Then, inhale, and come up again.

Benefits

    1. This asana helps to make the body extremely flexible. It thoroughly stretches the back and leg muscles.
    2. It helps to tone the abdominal area and get rid of belly fat.
    3. Since the abdominal organs get massaged, digestion is improved, and constipation is eliminated. This asana keeps stomach ailments at bay.
    4. The spine is flexed, and the nerves are toned.

Makarasana - To relax the Nervous System

This asana is a basic yoga pose. It quite literally means the Crocodile Pose. ‘Makara’ in Sanskrit means crocodile, ‘asana’ means pose. It is an ultimate relaxation pose.

How to do it?

    1. Lie flat on your stomach on an even floor, keeping your hands crossed under your forehead.
    2. Close your eyes. Stretch your legs as far as possible with your toes pointing outwards.
    3. Relax your body, as you breathe slowly and normally. Feel your body on the ground and relax every muscle in your body.
    4. Hold this position for two to five minutes, and then, bring your legs together.

Benefits

    1. Being a relaxation pose, it can be done between hectic asanas to give yourself a break, or when you are extremely tired in the course of the day.
    2. This asana thoroughly relaxes the back and the spine along with the nerves and muscles.

Dhanurasana - For stress release and relief from Menstrual Cramps

This asana is a basic yoga pose. This asana resembles a bow, and, therefore, is named so. ‘Dhanur’ in Sanskrit means bow, and ‘asana’ means pose. It is pronounced as dah-noo-rah-sah-nah.

How to do it?

    1. Lie flat on your stomach, keeping your feet hip-width apart and your arms beside your body.
    2. Now, gently fold your knees and hold your ankles.
    3. Inhale, and lift your chest and legs off the ground. Pull your legs back.
    4. Look straight and keep your face stress-free. A smile should help.
    5. Hold the pose as you concentrate on breathing. Your body should be as taut as a bow.
    6. As you get comfortable in the pose, breathe long and deep.
    7. About 15-20 seconds later, exhale and release the pose.

Benefits

    1. It helps to strengthen the back as well as the abdominal muscles.
    2. This asana helps to stimulate the reproductive organs.
    3. Practicing this asana helps to widen and open up the neck, chest, and shoulders.
    4. The legs and arm muscles are toned.
    5. Adds flexibility to the back.
    6. This asana is a great stress buster.
    7. Menstrual discomfort is relieved with regular practice.
    8. This asana also helps people with renal disorders.

Ardha Salabhasana - To stimulate digestive System and Nervous System

The name comes from a Sanskrit word that means grasshopper. The full pose resembles a grasshopper, and this one is a half pose that has an equal number of benefits.

How to do it?

    1. Lie flat on the ground with your stomach facing the floor. Rest your chin on the ground and place your palms under your thighs.
    2. Breathe in, and raise your right leg as high as possible.
    3. Hold the position for at least 30 seconds, while breathing normally.
    4. As you breathe out, bring your leg down.
    5. Repeat the same with your left leg.

Benefits

    1. This asana helps strengthen the shoulders and the arms. The back muscles and the abdominal muscles are fortified too.
    2. This asana stimulates the nervous system.
    3. This asana also helps to increase the appetite and streamline the digestive process.
    4. It balances out the functioning of the liver and the other abdominal organs.

Bhujangasana - Tp stimulate and increase Metabolism

The eighth pose of the 12 poses of the Surya Namaskar, Bhujangasana is also called the Cobra Pose. In this asana, the trunk and head resemble the raised hood of a cobra. ‘Bhujanga’ means cobra in Sanskrit.

How to do it?

    1. Lie flat on your stomach. Place your hands on the side, and ensure that your toes touch each other.
    2. Then, move your hands to the front, making sure they are at the shoulder level, and place your palms on the floor.
    3. Now, placing your body’s weight on your palms, slowly raise your head and trunk. Note that your arms should be bent at your elbows at this stage.
    4. You need to arch your neck backwards in an attempt to replicate the cobra with the raised hood.
    5. Hold the asana for a few seconds while breathing normally. Feel your stomach pressed against the floor. With practice, you should be able to hold the asana for up to two minutes.
    6. To release the pose, slowly bring your hands back to the sides, and rest your head on the ground by bringing your forehead in contact with the floor. Place your hands under your head. Then, slowly rest your head on one side and breathe.
    7. Repeat this pose thrice for best results.

Benefits

    1. The Bhujangasana affects the back and the abdomen. The muscles of the back and lower back are worked, and the spine’s flexibility is increased.
    2. It also tones the organs that lie in the lower abdomen.
    3. It stimulates the digestive, reproductive, and urinary systems.
    4. It also helps regulate metabolism, thus regulating the weight.
    5. The cobra pose also activates the Svadhisthana Chakra.

Utthanpadasana - To improve Blood Circulation

The name comes from the Sanskrit words ‘uttana’ meaning intense stretch, or straight, or stretched and ‘pada’ meaning leg. ‘Asana’ means posture or pose. Very simply, this asana involves the stretching of legs, and is a basic Yoga asana.

How to do it?

    1. Lie flat on the floor with your back facing the ground.
    2. Place your arms beside your body, making sure that your palms are flat on the floor. Keep your feet outstretched and your toes together.
    3. Raise both your legs until they are about 80 to 90 degrees high. Set your gaze on your big toes. Hold for about 30 seconds.
    4. Then, slowly bring your legs down, ensuring you do not jerk your legs.

Benefits

    1. It cures stomach disorders like indigestion, acidity, and constipation, and strengthens the abdominal organs.
    2. This asana helps to strengthen the thigh and hip muscles.
    3. It is a great asana for people suffering from arthritis, back pains, diabetes, and heart problems.
    4. This asana helps to tone the stomach muscles and reduces leg and belly fat.
    5. It enhances blood circulation all over the body.
    6. It also improves the function of the reproductive organs.

Virbhadrasana - To increase Stamina

Virabhadra is a mythological character created by Lord Shiva, and this pose derives its name from there. The Virabhadrasana I is an asana honoring the feats of a legendary warrior. Hence, it is also called the Warrior 1 pose. This asana is considered to be one of the most graceful postures in yoga, and it adds a whole lot of beauty to the workout.

How to do it?

    1. Stand erect and spread your legs about three to four feet apart. Your right foot should be in the front and the left foot behind.
    2. Now, turn your right foot outwards by 90 degrees and the left by 15 degrees, making sure the heel of the right foot is perfectly aligned with the center of the left foot.
    3. Lift your arms sideways until they reach the height of your shoulders. Your arms must be parallel to the ground, and your palms should be facing upwards.
    4. Exhale, and bend your right knee, such that your knee and ankle form a straight line. Make sure that your knee does not go ahead of your ankle.
    5. Now, turn your gaze to your right.
    6. As you move into the pose, stretch your arms further, and join your palms above your head. Look at your palms. Gently push your pelvis down.
    7. Hold the pose with the same determination as a warrior, and wear a smile on your face. Breathe normally and keep going down.
    8. Inhale and come up.
    9. Exhale, and gently bring your hands down from the sides.
    10. Repeat this pose on the left side, with your left leg in the front and the right one at the back.

Benefits

    1. This asana is known to strengthen and tone the lower back, the arms, and the legs.
    2. It helps to stabilize and balance the body as it increases the stamina.
    3. It is also a great asana for those with desk or sedentary jobs. It stimulates the metabolism as well as restores the spine.
    4. This asana helps ease out frozen shoulders.
    5. It also helps release stress from the shoulders almost immediately.
    6. This asana relaxes the mind and the body, spreading the notion of peace, courage, grace, and a sense of auspiciousness.
